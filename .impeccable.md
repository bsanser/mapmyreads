## Design Context

### Users
Avid readers (Goodreads and StoryGraph users) who want to visualize where in the world their books come from. They care about their reading life — this is a personal, meaningful thing to them, not a productivity tool. They use the app in a reflective mood: curious about patterns in what they've read, proud of how far they've travelled through literature.

### Brand Personality
Warm · Personal · Charming

The app should feel like a beloved personal project — the kind of thing someone made for themselves and decided to share. Not slick, not corporate. A curated object with character. Think: a beautifully annotated travel journal, or a hand-labeled map pinned above a desk.

Emotional goal: **delight and quiet pride**. Users should smile when they see their map.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm & Indie** — light mode only (for now).

- **What it should feel like:** A personal project with a curated, handcrafted quality. Imperfect in the right ways. Like a Letterboxd profile or a nice Notion page — personal but considered.
- **Palette:** No purple or indigo. Lean into warm tones: cream/off-white backgrounds, warm ink for text (never pure black), earthy accents — terracotta, amber, sand, or warm sage. Tinted neutrals throughout, never cold grays.
- **Typography:** The existing `font-mono` is used everywhere and creates an accidental developer-tool feel. Reserve mono for small metadata only (dates, ISBNs). Introduce a warm serif for display headings (the app name, hero headline) and a clean, slightly rounded sans for UI text.
- **Surfaces:** Off-white, not white. Warm paper-like backgrounds. Borders should be soft and warm-tinted, not gray-200.
- **What to keep:** The notebook-lines on book cards and the paperclip SVG are personality wins — they feel handcrafted. Keep them.
- **Anti-references:** No purple/indigo gradients. No cold glassmorphism. No generic SaaS dashboard feel. Avoid the "Inter + gray scale + purple accent" default.
- **Map:** The map is the hero on the main screen. Sidebars and overlays should feel like they belong to the same handcrafted world — warm paper tones, soft shadows.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over polish** — A slightly imperfect, curated feel beats a corporate-slick finish. Embrace personality details (the paperclip, the notebook lines) and extend that logic to the rest of the UI.
2. **Map is the hero** — The interactive world map is the centrepiece. Every other element should complement it, not compete. Sidebars and overlays should feel lightweight and secondary.
3. **Typography carries personality** — Thoughtful font choices communicate indie/literary quality more than anything else. Avoid mono for body text; use a warm serif for display and a clean sans for UI.
4. **Tinted, never cold** — All neutrals (backgrounds, borders, text) should carry a warm tint. No pure black, no cold gray-200, no default Tailwind grays unchanged.
5. **Delight in small moments** — Hover states, transitions, empty states, and micro-details are where the personality lives. Each one is an opportunity to make the user smile.
